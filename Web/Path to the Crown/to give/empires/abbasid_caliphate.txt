The Abbasid Caliphate, ruling from 750 to 1258 CE, succeeded the Umayyad Caliphate and is often considered the Golden Age of Islamic civilization. Centered in Baghdad, it fostered significant advancements in science, mathematics, philosophy, and the arts. The Abbasid period saw the flourishing of the House of Wisdom, a major intellectual center that preserved and translated ancient texts, contributing greatly to the preservation and advancement of human knowledge.
